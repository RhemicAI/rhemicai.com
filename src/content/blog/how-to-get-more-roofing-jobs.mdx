Roofing has a unique growth shape: long quiet stretches punctuated by storm-driven surges where demand spikes overnight and every roofer in the area is chasing the same roofs. Winning roofing jobs is about being ready to capture the surge faster than anyone else, and being found when homeowners go looking.

## The speed game

When a storm hits, homeowners call several roofers at once and go with whoever answers and shows up first. This is the most speed-sensitive trade there is. A missed call during a surge isn't one lost job, it's a high-ticket job that went to the roofer who picked up. Most home service businesses miss 20 to 40 percent of calls, and during a surge that rate gets worse exactly when the jobs are biggest.

So capture is the foundation:

- **Answer every call**, with a system that scales when the volume spikes, so a storm surge doesn't overwhelm one receptionist. See [the capture system for home services](/blog/stop-losing-leads-to-missed-calls).
- **Recover misses instantly** with a text back.
- **Respond and schedule the inspection fast**, while the homeowner is still deciding.

## Get found for both emergency and insurance queries

Roofing search splits into two high-value intents: urgent ("emergency roof repair near me") and insurance ("roofer that works with my insurance"). Homeowners increasingly ask AI assistants these exact questions, and the AI names only a few roofers.

To be one of them:

- **Google Business Profile** complete and active, with storm and insurance services listed.
- **Service pages** for repair, replacement, storm damage, and insurance claims, structured with LocalBusiness schema and answering the real questions, including how you handle claims. The how-to is in [how to show up in ChatGPT for your trade](/blog/how-to-show-up-in-chatgpt-for-your-trade).
- **Reviews**, which matter enormously for a high-trust, high-ticket purchase.

## Rethink lead aggregators

Roofing leads are expensive and high-value, which makes shared-lead platforms especially painful: the same homeowner is sold to several roofers, your close rate drops, and the cost per booked job climbs. Owned visibility plus fast capture usually beats it. We break down the tradeoff in [is Angi worth it in 2026](/blog/is-angi-worth-it-2026).

## Prove what worked

Storm spend is lumpy and easy to waste. Track cost per booked job by source so you know which channels actually produced signed contracts, not just calls. The method is in [closed-loop attribution for home services](/blog/closed-loop-attribution-for-home-services).

## The bottom line

Roofing jobs go to the company that is found for the right queries, answers every call during the surge, and responds before the competitor does. Build that system and storm season becomes a windfall instead of a scramble.
