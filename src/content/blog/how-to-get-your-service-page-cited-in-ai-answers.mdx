AI engines don't recommend pages, they cite sources. To be one, your service pages have to be the clearest, most trustworthy answer to the question a homeowner asked. Here's the page anatomy that gets you quoted, not skipped.

## Why citation is the goal

When ChatGPT, Perplexity, or a Google AI Overview answers "what does AC repair cost in my area" or "best emergency plumber near me," it synthesizes from sources it trusts and often links them. If your page is that source, you get named, linked, and visited at the moment of intent. If it isn't, a competitor's page is. So the work isn't "rank," it's "be the citable source." This is the applied version of [answer engine optimization for contractors](/blog/answer-engine-optimization-for-contractors).

## The anatomy of a citation-worthy page

**1. Answer first.** Open the page (and each section) with the direct answer, then explain. AI engines quote upfront, clear statements. If the answer is buried under three paragraphs of intro, they look elsewhere.

**2. Question-style headings.** Use H2s phrased the way people actually ask: "How much does a water heater replacement cost?" "How fast can you come out for an emergency?" This mirrors prompts and makes your answers easy to extract.

**3. Concrete specifics.** Real numbers, ranges, timelines, service areas, and process steps. "Most water heater replacements run X to Y and take about Z hours" is citable. "We offer quality service at competitive prices" is noise. Specificity signals expertise and gives the engine something to quote.

**4. Schema markup.** LocalBusiness and Service schema so the engine knows exactly what you do and where, plus FAQPage schema on Q&A sections. This is the machine-readable layer, detailed in [schema markup for local businesses](/blog/schema-markup-for-local-businesses).

**5. Real expertise.** First-hand operator knowledge, common scenarios, what to expect, mistakes to avoid. Genuine experience reads differently from recycled generic content, and engines increasingly favor it.

**6. Entity trust.** Consistent business name, address, and phone across the page, your profile, and directories, so the engine is confident it's all the same trustworthy business.

## What gets you skipped

The flip side, so you can audit your own pages:

- Vague, benefit-free marketing copy with no specifics.
- The answer buried below the fold.
- No schema, so the engine has to guess.
- Thin or recycled content with no real expertise.
- Inconsistent business details that undermine trust.

If your service pages look like that, they're invisible to AI no matter how nice they look to a human.

## A simple test

For each service page, ask: if an AI had to answer a homeowner's question using only this page, could it, clearly, with specifics, in the first few lines? If yes, you're citable. If it would have to dig or guess, rewrite it answer-first with real detail.

## The bottom line

Getting cited in AI answers is page craft: answer first, use question headings, include real specifics and schema, and show genuine expertise. Do that across your service pages and you become the source AI recommends, while generic competitor pages get passed over.
