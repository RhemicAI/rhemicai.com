Most advice on getting more HVAC jobs is a list of tactics: run ads, post on social, ask for reviews. Tactics aren't the problem. The problem is that HVAC growth is a system with three parts, and most companies pour effort into one while leaking at another.

The three parts: get found, answer and book, and prove what worked. Get all three right and the jobs compound. Here's the playbook.

## Part 1: Get found (and not just on Google)

A homeowner whose AC dies in July finds an HVAC company in one of a few ways now: Google search, the map pack, and increasingly by asking an AI assistant like ChatGPT or Google's AI for a recommendation. You need to show up across all of them.

The fundamentals that feed every surface at once:

- **A complete, active Google Business Profile.** Correct categories, hours, photos, and a steady flow of recent reviews. This drives the map pack and feeds AI recommendations.
- **Service pages structured for search and AI.** A real page for each service (repair, replacement, maintenance, emergency) that answers what it costs, how long it takes, and what's included, with LocalBusiness schema so machines can read it.
- **Reviews.** Volume, recency, and sentiment. Every surface weighs them.

The big shift: homeowners increasingly ask AI to name the best HVAC company nearby, and the AI only names a few. The full how-to is in [how to show up in ChatGPT for your trade](/blog/how-to-show-up-in-chatgpt-for-your-trade). The competitive set is still thin, so moving now is a real advantage.

And a note on lead aggregators: Angi and HomeAdvisor are rented demand that resells the same lead to your competitors. Building owned visibility means homeowners find and choose you directly, which is cheaper and more durable.

## Part 2: Answer and book every call

This is where most HVAC companies quietly lose the most money. HVAC demand spikes after hours, on weekends, and during heat waves, exactly when your techs are on jobs and can't grab the phone. Most home service businesses miss 20 to 40 percent of calls, and most callers who hit voicemail never call back. They call the next company.

The fix is a capture system:

- **Answer every call**, including after hours and overflow, with a system rather than hoping someone's free. Compare your options in [AI receptionist vs answering service vs CSR](/blog/ai-receptionist-vs-answering-service-vs-csr).
- **Recover every miss** with an instant text back, so a missed ring becomes a live text conversation.
- **Respond fast and book on the spot.** Speed is the single biggest lever on booking rate.

If you are generating leads but not booking them, this is almost always why. Run the dollar math in [how much revenue missed calls are costing your business](/blog/how-much-revenue-are-missed-calls-costing-your-business). Fixing capture books more jobs this month without spending another dollar on marketing.

## Part 3: Prove what worked, then scale it

Once you are found and booking, the last step is knowing which marketing actually produced the jobs, so you scale what works and cut what doesn't.

That means measuring cost per booked job, not cost per lead, and connecting each booked job back to its source with call tracking and a record of outcomes. The full method is in [closed-loop attribution for home services](/blog/closed-loop-attribution-for-home-services), and the paid-specific view is in [Google Ads cost per lead for HVAC](/blog/google-ads-cost-per-lead-for-hvac).

Without this, you keep spending on channels that feel busy but don't book. With it, every dollar goes where it produces revenue.

## Put it together

Here's the order for an HVAC company starting now:

1. **Fix capture first.** Answer every call and respond fast. Fastest ROI, no extra ad spend.
2. **Build owned visibility.** Google Business Profile, structured service pages, AI search readiness, reviews.
3. **Instrument attribution.** Track cost per booked job and scale what works.

Do them in that order and you stop leaking the demand you already have, then grow the demand you own, then spend only on what proves out.

## The bottom line

More HVAC jobs don't come from one clever tactic. They come from being found everywhere homeowners look, answering every call that results, and proving which source paid off. Run all three as one system and you out-earn competitors who only do one.
