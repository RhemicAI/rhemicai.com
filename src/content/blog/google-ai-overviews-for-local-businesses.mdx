Google AI Overviews changed what the top of a search result looks like. Instead of ten links, a homeowner increasingly sees an AI-written summary that answers the question and names a few businesses or sources. For local service businesses, getting included in that summary is the new front row.

## What AI Overviews are

An AI Overview is the AI-generated answer Google places above the traditional results on many searches. It reads relevant sources, synthesizes an answer, and links out. For a query like "best water heater repair in my area" or "how much does a new roof cost," the overview often frames the homeowner's entire first impression before they scroll.

For local intent, this matters because the overview can shape which businesses get considered at all. If you're cited or surfaced, you're in the conversation. If not, you're below the fold of an answer many people won't scroll past.

## How they pick sources

The reassuring part: AI Overviews are grounded in Google's core Search and local systems. Google has been explicit that foundational SEO still drives its AI features and that there's no special markup required to appear. So the inputs are the ones you already control:

- **Strong foundational SEO** and technically healthy, crawlable pages.
- **A complete, active Google Business Profile** for local relevance.
- **Structured data** (LocalBusiness, FAQPage) so your content is machine-readable.
- **Answer-first content** that directly answers the question, making your page quotable.
- **Reviews and trust signals** that support a confident recommendation.

If that list looks familiar, it should. ## The zero-click reality, and why local is different

AI Overviews can reduce clicks for purely informational searches, because the answer is right there. That's real. But local service intent behaves differently: a homeowner who needs a plumber isn't reading for fun, they're choosing who to contact. Being named or cited in the overview for that query drives high-intent calls, not just impressions.

So the play is two-fold: be the source the overview cites, and make sure the contacts it sends you convert. Visibility that doesn't capture is wasted, the trap in [why you rank but get no calls](/blog/you-rank-on-google-but-get-no-calls).

## What to do now

1. **Get the fundamentals right**, profile, schema, answer-first pages, reviews.
2. **Answer the questions homeowners actually ask** on your pages, so you're the citable source.
3. **Track AI-driven visibility** as Google's Search Console reporting for AI features matures, and watch which pages earn it.
4. **Capture aggressively**, so the high-intent contacts overviews produce become booked jobs.

## The bottom line

Google AI Overviews are now part of how local searches resolve. You get included the same way you win the rest of search: strong fundamentals, structured data, answer-first content, and trust, made legible to Google's AI. Do it now while the competitive set is still thin.
