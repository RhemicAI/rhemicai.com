If you run an agency for home service clients, you've lived this: you generate solid leads, the client's phone goes to voicemail, no jobs book, and your campaigns get blamed for a problem that wasn't yours. White-label capture and attribution fixes that, and turns a liability into a margin line.

## The gap that's killing your ROI

Most agencies stop at lead generation. You drive calls and form fills, then hand off. But what happens after the handoff decides whether your work produces revenue, and you don't control it:

- The client misses the call (on a job, after hours, overflow).
- The client takes a message instead of booking.
- No one tracks which booked jobs came from your campaigns.

So even great campaigns can look like failures, and the client churns. You did your job and still lost the account. The retention case for fixing this is in [how agencies prove ROI to home service clients](/blog/how-agencies-prove-roi-to-home-service-clients).

## What white-label capture and attribution adds

By offering capture and attribution under your own brand, you extend from "we get you leads" to "we get you booked jobs and prove it":

- **Capture:** call answering, missed-call text back, and fast response, so the leads you generate actually convert. The system is in [stop losing leads to missed calls](/blog/stop-losing-leads-to-missed-calls).
- **Attribution:** closed-loop tracking that ties booked jobs back to your campaigns, so you report revenue, not clicks. The method is in [closed-loop attribution for home services](/blog/closed-loop-attribution-for-home-services).
- **Your brand:** the client experiences it as your agency's service, deepening the relationship.

## Why this is good business for the agency

Three wins:

**1. It protects your reported ROI.** When you control capture, the leads you generate don't die on voicemail. Your numbers reflect your actual work, so good campaigns look good.

**2. It boosts retention.** Clients who can see booked jobs and revenue from your work don't churn. Capture plus attribution is the proof that keeps them.

**3. It adds recurring margin.** Capture and attribution are sticky, recurring services. You move from project-based lead gen to a fuller retainer with more value and more lock-in.

## How to position it to clients

Don't sell "software." Sell the outcome: "We don't just get you leads, we make sure they become booked jobs, and we prove which marketing produced your revenue." That's the full-funnel promise contractors actually want, and few agencies offer. It differentiates you from every competitor still reporting clicks.

## The bottom line

Lead generation alone leaves your ROI at the mercy of your client's phone. White-label capture and attribution closes the loop: the leads you generate convert, you prove the revenue, the client sees your brand, and you add sticky recurring margin. It's how a lead-gen agency becomes a results agency.
